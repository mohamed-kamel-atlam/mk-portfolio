## Purpose

This document records the products that inspire the portfolio's visual and
interaction quality bar, named in [BRAND.md → Visual Direction](../product/BRAND.md)
and [DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md → Inspiration](../design/DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md). For each,
it states the *specific* quality this project draws from it — not a vague
admiration, but a concrete influence on a design or engineering decision.

---

## Scope

**In scope.** The five inspiration products and the single, specific quality
each contributes; the explicit *inspiration, never imitation* boundary.

**Out of scope.** Copying any of these products' visual identity, layout, or
components; and general design theory (see [books.md](./books.md)) or technical
standards (see [articles.md](./articles.md)).

---

## Inspiration, never imitation

[DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md → Inspiration](../design/DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md) states the rule
plainly: *"The goal is inspiration, never imitation."*
[BRAND.md → Visual Direction](../product/BRAND.md) reinforces it: design should
feel inspired by these products *"without copying any existing design."*

What that means in practice:

- We draw a **principle or quality** — restraint, focus, command-driven flow —
  and express it through *this* project's own design system and tokens.
- We never lift a layout, a component, a color palette, or a brand mark.
- The test is [BRAND → Brand Mission](../product/BRAND.md): prove excellence
  *through the product itself*, not by resembling admired products. A portfolio
  that looked like a clone of Linear would fail its own brand promise.

The inspirations are a **quality bar**, not a template.

## The inspirations

### Vercel — performance and restraint

What we draw: an obsession with **performance as a first-class product feature**
and a **restrained, content-first aesthetic** where nothing decorative competes
with the work. This underpins QAT-1 (*performance by default*) in
[ARCHITECTURE → §1](../engineering/ARCHITECTURE.md) and the
[Design Language](../design/DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md) priority of content and
typography over decoration.

Primary source: <https://vercel.com>

### Linear — focus and motion discipline

What we draw: **focus** — a calm, uncluttered interface that shows only what
matters — and **motion discipline**, where animation is fast, purposeful, and
never decorative. Directly informs
[BRAND → Motion Principles](../product/BRAND.md) and
[DESIGN_LANGUAGE → Motion Philosophy](../design/DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md): motion
exists to explain and to aid navigation, never to entertain.

Primary source: <https://linear.app>

### Raycast — command-driven UX

What we draw: a **command-driven, keyboard-first interaction model** that makes
power-user speed feel effortless. This is the north star for the planned
Command Menu ([PRD → FR-011](../product/PRODUCT_REQUIREMENTS.md), a Version 2
feature) and reinforces the [UX Principles](../product/BRAND.md) of speed,
focus, and discoverability.

Primary source: <https://raycast.com>

### Huly — density done calmly

What we draw: the ability to present **information-dense, structured content
without feeling cluttered** — generous structure and hierarchy holding a lot of
material calmly. Relevant to the rich project detail pages
([PRD → FR-005](../product/PRODUCT_REQUIREMENTS.md)) and the engineering
documentation, where depth must not become noise. Supports
[DESIGN_LANGUAGE → Layout Philosophy](../design/DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md).

Primary source: <https://huly.io>

### OpenAI — confident minimalism

What we draw: **confident minimalism** — large, clear typography, generous
whitespace, and an authoritative-yet-calm tone that signals seriousness without
loudness. This shapes the [Typography Philosophy](../design/DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md)
(typography as the primary design element) and the
[Brand Personality](../product/BRAND.md) target of *premium, intelligent, calm*.

Primary source: <https://openai.com>
