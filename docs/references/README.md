## Purpose

This section is the project's **curated external knowledge base**: the outside
sources — products, books, and articles — that inform the engineering and design
standards adopted throughout the documentation. It exists so that the standards
in the [engineering](../engineering/ARCHITECTURE.md) and
[design](../design/DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md) documents are traceable to their
influences, not asserted in a vacuum.

The references are an **influence trail**, not a bibliography for its own sake.
Each entry earns its place by having shaped a real decision in this project, and
each says *why* it is relevant here. This mirrors the project's own value of
[Continuous Learning](../product/BRAND.md): the standards did not appear
fully-formed; they were learned from the best available sources.

---

## Scope

**In scope.** A curated, deliberately small set of high-signal external
references, organized by kind: product inspirations, books, and articles /
official documentation.

**Out of scope.** Internal sources of truth (the product, design, and
engineering documents — those are linked from each document's own *Related
Documents*); exhaustive reading lists; and any resource that has not actually
informed a decision here. Quality over quantity: an unused reference is
clutter.

## Organization

The section is split by the *kind* of source, because each kind is used
differently:

| File | Contains | Used for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [inspiration.md](./inspiration.md) | The products named in [BRAND.md](../product/BRAND.md) and [DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md](../design/DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md) — Vercel, Linear, Raycast, Huly, OpenAI. | The *visual and interaction bar* the portfolio aims at — and the explicit boundary that this is inspiration, never imitation. |
| [books.md](./books.md) | Foundational books on software design, UI, accessibility, and craft. | The *principles* behind the architecture, component, and design decisions. |
| [articles.md](./articles.md) | Official documentation and canonical articles (Next.js, React, web.dev, WCAG, MDN, Tailwind). | The *technical standards* the implementation follows. |

### Inclusion criteria

Every entry must be:

- **Real and verifiable** — a genuine, well-known product, book, or document,
  cited with a primary source (official site or canonical URL). Nothing is
  invented.
- **Relevant** — accompanied by one line explaining what this project draws from
  it. If the "why" cannot be stated, the entry does not belong here.
- **Primary** — link the source itself (official site, publisher, spec), never a
  mirror, aggregator, or paywalled repost.
